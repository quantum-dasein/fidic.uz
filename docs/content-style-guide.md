# FIDIC.uz content style guide

## Editorial position

FIDIC.uz should read like a practical knowledge hub written by project people who have seen contracts fail in the field. The tone is expert, calm and commercially useful. It should not sound like a generic legal memo or an AI-generated explainer.

Primary audience:

- contractors preparing tenders or claims;
- employers and PIUs administering MDB-funded works;
- engineers, contract managers, QS teams and consultants;
- lawyers who need project context rather than abstract clause summaries.

## Voice rules

- Open with a real project tension, not with a textbook definition.
- Prefer concrete nouns: notice register, Contract Data, programme update, IPC file, Engineer instruction, DAAB visit.
- Use short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph.
- Keep English contract terms when they are search terms or industry terms: Contract Data, Particular Conditions, Notice of Claim, fully detailed claim, Contract Management Plan.
- Use cautious legal language: "usually", "often", "may", "depends on the contract wording", "requires project-specific review".
- Do not promise outcomes: no "this guarantees recovery", "this wins the dispute", "this is always invalid".
- Do not copy long FIDIC wording. Explain the mechanism in original language.

Avoid:

- "In this article we will consider..."
- "It is important to note that..." unless the sentence really earns it.
- generic AI phrasing such as "plays a crucial role", "in today's dynamic environment", "comprehensive overview";
- overlong lists that read like keyword stuffing;
- unsupported local-law statements about Uzbekistan, tax, licences, TIAC, PE, enforceability or sanctions.

## Article structure

Recommended structure for practical articles:

1. Sharp opening: project pain or decision moment.
2. Why the topic matters commercially.
3. Practical framework or workflow.
4. Typical mistakes.
5. Internal links to existing FIDIC.uz materials.
6. FAQ with 3 questions.
7. Soft Bridge Consult CTA.
8. Sources and further reading.

## SEO rules

- One primary target keyword per article.
- H1 must be unique and should include the primary keyword naturally.
- Description should be under 170 characters when possible and sell the practical value.
- Tags must match the existing schema and become article keywords in structured data.
- Use long-tail headings, but do not repeat the same keyword mechanically.
- Every advanced follow-up must link to the closest existing base article to avoid cannibalization.

## Duplicate rules

- Do not create another general "what is FIDIC" article.
- Do not create another general Rainbow Series, Silver Book, Engineer role, Variations, EOT, 28-day notice, Performance Security, Taking-Over/DNP, Pink Book, Clause 14, certification, Exceptional Events or Emerald Book article.
- If a topic is close to an existing article, narrow it to a specific user task: pre-bid review, register design, evidence pack, DAAB site visit, ESHS reporting, Contract Data audit.
- New slugs, H1s and target keywords must be unique.

## Source discipline

Preferred sources:

- official FIDIC pages and official publications;
- World Bank, ADB, EBRD, AIIB, IsDB procurement documents and guidance;
- SCL Delay and Disruption Protocol;
- DRBF resources for dispute boards;
- official Uzbekistan sources only when local law is discussed.

Do not rely on ResearchGate as a main source if an official or professional source exists.

When sources are paid or copyright-protected, cite the publication title but do not reproduce protected wording.
